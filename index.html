The Financial Engine

TBM & FinOps: prove value in Euros

Two views on one question: where does the money go, and is it worth it?

Macro View: TBM

Where every Euro goes

Map IT costs to business units. No more black-box budgets.

Example: “IT cost for dental product dev = CHF 2.4M/year, split by infrastructure, apps, and people.”

Micro View: FinOps

The cost of one croissant

Know the unit cost of every IT service and AI action. Compare it to the manual alternative.

AI example: One AI-written summary costs 12 cents. Doing it by hand costs 4.50. That saves money. Scale it. One AI quality check costs 8.20. Doing it by hand costs 3. That loses money. Stop it.

“TBM tells the board where the money goes. FinOps tells the teams whether it is worth spending.”

C | AI Orchestration + FinOps Gate

Enable AI safely: know what leaves the building

AI Radar

Know what tools people already use

Who uses Perplexity, ChatGPT, Copilot? What company data goes in? Today nobody tracks this.

Data Classification

Not all data can leave the building

Green: public info, OK to use. Yellow: internal, needs approval. Red: formulas, patient data, pricing. Never.

Approved Tool List

One short list instead of 50 random subscriptions

Approved tools get enterprise licenses with data agreements. Unapproved tools get flagged at procurement.

FinOps Gate

The money check before we scale

Does this AI save more than it costs to run? If yes, scale. If no, stop.

Shadow Demand: The Global Challenge

Private jets landing outside the system

We don't say no. We say how. You don't put brakes on a car to drive slower. You put brakes on a car so you can drive faster.

Fast Clearance Lane

Make the official way quicker than the workaround

Regional IT partners can approve small, low-risk requests on the spot. No need to wait for headquarters.

Turn On the Radar

See what tools and AI people are already using

We check all subscriptions, software, and AI tools across regions. Shadow AI is growing fast. If we do not track it, we cannot secure it or learn from it.

Procurement Integration

Catch shadow tools when the bill comes in

Finance flags unknown subscriptions at renewal time. Every tool, including AI services, needs an owner and a place in the portfolio.

Delegated Control Towers

Local people who follow global rules

Each region gets an enablement lead who can act fast, but within the same framework as everyone else.

300-Day Plan + Quick Wins

Listen → Launch → Stabilize

Day 1 – 60

Listen

  • 30+ stakeholder interviews across HQ and regions
  • Visit 8–10 of the 56 subsidiaries in person
  • Map all active projects, processes, and AI tools
  • Find shadow demand: who buys what outside IT?
  • Build trust before building systems
Day 60 – 100

Launch + 3 Quick Wins

  • Front Door live. Every request answered in 48h
  • Portfolio reset: cut or pause 20% of low-value projects
  • Radar sweep: inventory 100% of subscriptions and shadow AI tools
  • Demand Board meets bi-weekly. First 5 project requests ranked by business impact, not by who asked loudest
  • Start TBM: tag every IT cost line to a business unit
Day 100 – 300

Stabilize, Embed & Grow

  • Governance cadence: 100% on schedule, no skipped meetings
  • 3+ AI pilots through proper gates into production
  • TBM dashboard live. Leadership sees costs anytime
  • FinOps practice launched: unit costs tracked for top 10 services
  • Gamify results: recognize teams with best value-to-cost ratio
  • 50/30/20 portfolio view delivered to the board
Taking People on the Journey

How I work with stakeholders

Business Partners

They are co-designers, not customers

I involve departments from the start. They help shape the system, so they actually want to use it. If people feel heard, they show up.

IT Team

I enable experts, not replace them

The existing teams are the experts. I bring structure and clarity. They bring the knowledge. My role is to support them, not to step on their toes.

Communication

When something works, everyone should know

Regular updates, no surprises. When the Front Door cuts response time, we share that story. Upward to leadership, sideways to peers.

Change Philosophy

Small wins build trust faster than big plans

I do not force change on people. I show results first, then they want to join. Pull, not push. That is how I have always worked.
